Dal Bati Recipe

Dal Baati is a quintessential dish from Rajasthan, India, comprising lentil curry (dal) paired with baked wheat dumplings (baati). Here's how you can prepare this traditional meal at home:

Ingredients

For Baati

  1. Wheat flour - 2 cups
  2. Semolina (sooji) - 1/2 cup
  3. Clarified butter (ghee) - 1/2 cup
  4. Yogurt - 1/2 cup
  5. Carom seeds (ajwain) - 1 tsp
  6. Salt - to taste
  7. Water - as needed

For Dal

  1. Split pigeon peas (toor dal) - 1 cup
  2. Onion (finely chopped) - 1 medium
  3. Tomato (finely chopped) - 1 medium
  4. Green chilies (slit) - 2
  5. Ginger (grated) - 1 tsp
  6. Garlic (minced) - 1 tsp
  7. Turmeric powder - 1/2 tsp
  8. Red chili powder - 1 tsp
  9. Coriander powder - 1 tsp
  10. Garam masala - 1/2 tsp
  11. Asafoetida (hing) - a pinch
  12. Mustard seeds - 1 tsp
  13. Cumin seeds - 1 tsp
  14. Curry leaves - a few
  15. Coriander leaves (chopped) - 2 tbsp
  16. Oil - 2 tbsp
  17. Salt - to taste

Instructions

For Baati

  1. Prepare the dough: Mix wheat flour, semolina, carom seeds, salt, and ghee in a bowl. Add yogurt and knead into a stiff dough using water.
  2. Shape the baatis: Divide the dough into equal portions and shape them into round balls. Flatten slightly to form discs.
  3. Bake the baatis: Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C) and bake the baatis for 30-40 minutes until golden brown.
  4. Crush and soak: Crush the baatis, pour ghee over them, and soak in warm dal before serving.

For Dal

  1. Pressure cook dal: Pressure cook toor dal with turmeric powder, salt, and water until soft and mushy.
  2. Prepare tempering: Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, hing, curry leaves, green chilies, ginger, and garlic. Sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add onions and tomatoes: Add chopped onions and tomatoes. Cook until soft and mushy.
  4. Season the dal: Add red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Garnish and serve: Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with baatis.

Enjoy the authentic flavors of Rajasthan with this delicious Dal Bati recipe!
