Paneer Sabji is a popular North Indian dish featuring paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a flavorful onion-tomato gravy. Here's a simple recipe to prepare this delicious dish at home:

Ingredients:

  1. Paneer (cubed) - 250g
  2. Onions (finely chopped) - 2 medium
  3. Tomatoes (finely chopped) - 2 medium
  4. Ginger-Garlic Paste - 1 tbsp
  5. Green Chilies (slit) - 2
  6. Turmeric Powder - 1/2 tsp
  7. Red Chili Powder - 1 tsp
  8. Coriander Powder - 1 tsp
  9. Garam Masala - 1/2 tsp
  10. Kasuri Methi (dried fenugreek leaves) - 1 tbsp
  11. Coriander Leaves (chopped) - 2 tbsp
  12. Oil - 2 tbsp
  13. Salt - to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Spices : Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Cook Onions: Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown
  3. Add Ginger-Garlic Paste: Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add Tomato Puree: Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  5. Add Spices: Mix in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  6. Optional Creaminess: If using, add fresh cream or yogurt to the gravy and mix well. Cook for 2 minutes
  7. Add Paneer: Gently add the paneer cubes to the gravy. Stir carefully to coat the paneer with the masala.
  8. Simmer: Cover and let the sabji simmer for 5 minutes on low heat, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors.
  9. Finish Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Serve hot with roti, naan, or steamed rice.
